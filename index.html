Solo - The Hohn Family Card Game

History of the game

Solo originated in Germany as a simplified version of l'Hombre; a card game that had its beginnings in 17th century Spain. Similar games to this are Solo Whist, an English version of Solo and Six-bid Solo. The version of Solo explained below came from my Hohn ancestors in Germany and the game emigrated with them to South Dakota where they settled, and then on to the west coast of the U.S.

Number of Players

Solo is a game played with four to six players. Game play starts to the dealers immediate left unless there are five or six players. When there are five players, the dealer will sit out of the hand. If there is a sixth player, the dealer and the player immediately to the dealers left will sit out of the hand.

Game Explanation

The game uses 32 cards from a standard card deck consisting of A, K, Q, J, 10, 9, 8, 7 in each suit. Each player is dealt 8 cards and is ultimately playing for themselves, though temporary patnerships are created by bidding. The game is over when a player(s) wins the amount of tricks that were bid at the begining of the hand, or is not able to reach that amount. Card play follows the standard heirarchy with a few exceptions, which will be explained below in more detail.

Dealing begins to the dealers left. A 3/2/3 dealing scheme or any personal preference can be incorporated to distribute the cards.

Card Heirarchy

As mentioned above, only cards Ace through 7 are used in this game. The game follows standard heirarchy with the following exceptions:

Trump Cards
Trump cards are used similar to how they are played in Spades. Any card that is designated as trump is ranked higher than all other non-trump cards. i.e. If spades are trump, a 9 of spades will beat an ace of any other suit.

Below is a list of trump cards by suit.

Clubs (highest value): ♣ Q, ♣ 7, ♠ Q, ♣ A, ♣ K, ♣ J, ♣ 10, ♣ 9, ♣ 8.
Spades: ♣ Q, ♠ 7, ♠ Q, ♠ A, ♠ K, ♠ J, ♠ 10, ♠ 9, ♠ 8.
Hearts: ♣ Q, ♥ 7, ♠ Q, ♥ A, ♥ K, ♥ Q, ♥ J, ♥10, ♥ 9, ♥ 8.
Diamonds: ♣ Q, ♦ 7, ♠ Q, ♦ A, ♦ K, ♦ Q, ♦ J, ♦ 10, ♦ 9, ♦ 8.

In all four suit structures, the queen of clubs is the highest, the 7 of trump second, and the queen of spades is third. These cards are usually referred to as the Old, the Spits, and the Blue, respectively.

     Hint: There are different amounts of trump cards depending on the suit. Clubs and spades have nine trump; hearts and diamonds have 10 trump.

Bidding

Bidding begins with the player to the dealer's left. A bid is stating what type of hand the bidder wishes to play. Higher value bids override lower ones.

There are two main types of bids:

Solo
A solo bid means the player will play against all three other players and attempt to reach the number of tricks that were bid at the begining of the hand.
A player may bid 5, 6, or 7 tricks for a solo hand. If 5 tricks are bid, clubs must be the trump suit. For 6 and 7 trick bids, any suit may be trump.
Partner
A partner bid is when a player cannot win enough tricks to play solo, and will request a partner, usually done by stating the suit of an ace*. i.e. player bids 6 spades with the Heart Ace. The bidder is stating that he or she will win a minimum of six tricks with the help of the person holding the Ace of Hearts.
* If the bidder has all four aces, he or she can ask for the king. This needs to be stated during the bid.
A player may bid 6 or 7 tricks for a partner hand. Any suit may be played.

Once a player decides on what to bid, they use the following phrases. The list increases in higher value bids.

Pass
Pass indicates you do not want to make a bid on the current hand.
Play
Play signifies that you are willing to make a bid without committing to a suit or bid number.
6 or 7
A player can bid 6 or 7 tricks depending on the strength of their hand.
Clubs
The Clubs suit outranks the other suits in Solo. If there are multiple bids of equal tricks, a player can state they are calling Clubs as the trump suit. In order to outbid clubs, a player must call for a higher value bid or go solo.

Example: If player #2 bids 6, player #3 must bid 6 clubs or 7 of any suit or solo.

Solo
Solo indicates you will be playing against the other three players.

Below are the different types of bids valued from lowest to highest:

  • Bid of 6 using Spades, Hearts, or Diamonds
  • Bid of 7 using Spades, Hearts, or Diamonds
  • Bid of 6 Clubs
  • Bid of 7 Clubs
  • Bid of 5 Club solo
  • Bid of 6 solo using Spades, Hearts, or Diamonds
  • Bid of 7 solo using Spades, Hearts, or Diamonds
  • Bid of 6 Club solo
  • Bid of 7 Club solo

Partner

The player that makes the highest bid will call for a partner when not going solo. The bidder states which ace (not one in their hand) they want for partner (the ace can not be the trump suit). The player with the ace must play the ace the first time that suit is played during the hand.

Queens

Queens is called out when a player has both the Queens of clubs and spades. If the bidder can go solo, do not call out queens. If the bidder can not go solo, he or she will state that they have the queens. The bidder will call an ace (not one in their hand) as partner. The player with the ace will bid the number of tricks and trump suit*.

* An unwritten rule of the game during Queens play is the bidder will call for an ace that is opposite in color of what he or she wants for trump. i.e. The bidder calls for the Ace of hearts as a partner. The player with the Ace of hearts should typically bid diamonds as the trump suit. This does depend on the strength of that suit, though.

Playing with money

If the players choose to play with money, each losing partner will pay one of the winning partners. If solo is bid, the winner will receive the appropriate amount from each player. If the player who bid solo loses, he or she must pay each player. Listed below are the values of each hand:

  • Bid of 6 using Spades, Hearts, or Diamonds - $0.25
  • Bid of 7 using Spades, Hearts, or Diamonds - $0.75
  • Bid of 6 Clubs - $0.50
  • Bid of 7 Clubs - $1.00
  • Bid of 5 Club solo - $0.50
  • Bid of 6 solo using Spades, Hearts, or Diamonds - $0.75
  • Bid of 7 solo using Spades, Hearts, or Diamonds - $1.00
  • Bid of 6 Club solo - $1.25
  • Bid of 7 Club solo - $1.50

Playing through

Once the bidder or bidding team reaches the amount of tricks that were bid at the beginning of the hand, the bidder or the bidders partner has the option to continue the hand. If it is decided to "play through," and the bidders win the hand, the amount paid out is doubled. If the bidders do not reach 7 tricks, they must pay the other players the amounts listed under "playing with money."
